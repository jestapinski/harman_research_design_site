Project Summary

To date, much of the work on vehicle autonomy involves proving the feasibility of the technology and ensuring safety and reliability. While stakeholders such as regulatory organizations, manufacturers and insurance companies have investigated the impact of autonomous vehicles (AV) on policy and business models, there has been less focus on the implications to human experience. A more complete understanding of human preferences for interacting with autonomous vehicles is needed to inform both the technical development of these systems and business decisions about how they create value.

Harman International

Harman International designs and engineers connected products and solutions for automakers, consumers, and enterprises worldwide. This includes connected car systems, audio and visual products, and enterprise automation solutions. Harman serves the world’s automakers with embedded infotainment, telematics, connected safety and security capabilities.

Where We Started

With Harman, we had the opportunity to explore and improve the user experience of autonomous vehicles. The domain is new, vast, and there are many avenues we could have taken, but we were motivated to improve on the autonomous driving experience for those who would be effected most, the driver. The best experience is achieved for the user when they are empowered with agency throughout the drive. We focused our efforts on giving drivers control of their vehicle.

This is accomplished by human decision making and control in collaboration with the vehicle’s artificial intelligence. We believe the best AV driving experience is achieved when the driver can feel, and maintain, a sense of agency throughout their drive.

Our Solution

With our direction in mind, we designed a curved interface that is implemented within the vehicle dashboard. This product exclusively provides the driver different aspects of control in unique situations during their autonomous driving experience.
